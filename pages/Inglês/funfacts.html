Curiosities

Now that we understand the structure and basic functions of the animal cell, it's interesting to note that it also holds several curiosities that make it even more fascinating. Some details go beyond what we learn in books and show how complex and surprising cellular life is. Take a look:

  1. Did you know that there are more cells in your body than there are stars in our galaxy?
  2. The animal cell was discovered in 1665 by Robert Hooke.
  3. Cells die, but some don't; these are the ones that give rise to cancer.
  4. If the DNA of a human cell were completely uncoiled, it would measure about 2 meters.
  5. There are cells that are visible to the naked eye, like an egg yolk, which is an unfertilized egg!
  6. Cells can see! Some have a type of "eye" that allows them to detect information from a certain distance.
  7. There are cells that eat your brain and control your mind! These are Toxoplasma, which causes behavioral changes in its hosts.
  8. There are more bacteria in the body than human cells.
  9. The first cells to exist were prokaryotes! These are nothing more than cells that lack a nucleus.
  10. Cells commit suicide; they self-destruct through a process called apoptosis.
  11. Some animals have cells that emit light, such as fireflies and some deep-sea fish (these fish live at very great depths where sunlight cannot reach).
  12. The cells in our bodies communicate with each other through chemical signals, such as hormones and neurotransmitters.
