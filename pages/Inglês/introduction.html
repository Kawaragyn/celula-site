Introduction to the Cell

Cells are microscopic units that make up all living beings, including us, humans. However, there are various types of them, and today we will talk about one: the animal cell.

This cell is the most important for animals (including humans), as it is the one that forms everything we are physically. Many of them come together to form tissues, these tissues form organs, skin, hair, and much more!

An interesting fact about the animal cell is that it is Eukaryotic. But what does that mean? Simply put, it means that it has a nucleus inside, making it more complex than those that do not – prokaryotes. However, it is important to remember that eukaryotes are not only found in animals but also in plants, which are plant cells!

Although they are similar in some aspects, prokaryotic, animal, and plant cells have important differences. See below a table comparing these three:
